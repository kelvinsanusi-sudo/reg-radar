AI Regulation

Government trend & party positions, plus tracked documents in this area
The UK government has shifted from proposing binding regulation on large AI model developers toward a sector-based regulatory approach using existing regulators. As of April 2026, the government's priorities focus on supporting British AI companies and establishing international standards for AI deployment, with no standalone AI Bill yet passed.
Labour

Labour's 2024 manifesto pledged binding regulation on the most powerful AI model developers, but by February 2025 pivoted to regulating most AI systems at the point of use through existing sector regulators rather than a single AI law. Tech Secretary Liz Kendall outlined two priorities in April 2026: supporting British AI companies and collaborating internationally on AI deployment standards.
