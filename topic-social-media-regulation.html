Social Media Regulation

Government trend & party positions, plus tracked documents in this area
UK parties are converging on protecting children from social media risks, though with different emphasis on enforcement mechanisms. There is notable variation between parties on the level of regulatory intervention required, from light-touch approaches to more interventionist frameworks addressing illegal content and algorithmic transparency.
Labour

Labour proposes comprehensive social media regulation addressing online crime, hate, extremism, fraud, gambling, and deepfakes. However, the party shows internal division on these proposals, with 107 Labour MPs abstaining on a House of Lords amendment on social media regulation.

Conservative

The Conservative position focuses specifically on restricting children's access to social media and implementing statutory school phone bans.

Reform UK

Reform UK supports child-friendly restricted phones and under-16 school phone bans, but broadly opposes mandating algorithm disclosure to regulators, reflecting a free-market, light-touch regulatory approach.

Green Party

No clear position found in research.

Liberal Democrats

The Liberal Democrats propose establishing a new 'Online Crime Agency' to tackle illegal content including fraud, revenge porn, and incitement to violence.
