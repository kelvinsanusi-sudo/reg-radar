Online Safety

Government trend & party positions, plus tracked documents in this area
There is broad cross-party support for building on the Online Safety Act 2023, with Labour and the Conservatives both proposing expansions to address emerging harms. However, Reform UK represents a significant outlier, pledging to scrap the Act entirely on free speech grounds while paradoxically supporting child-focused restrictions.
Labour

Labour wants to build on the Online Safety Act 2023 with further measures targeting online crime, hate, extremism, fraud, gambling, and deepfakes. They also propose giving coroners access to tech firms' data following a child's death.

Conservative

The Conservatives also support building on the Online Safety Act 2023, with a focused agenda on restricting children's access to social media and placing school phone bans on a statutory footing.

Reform UK

Reform UK pledged on 28 July 2025 to scrap the Online Safety Act entirely, characterising it as a threat to free speech. The party separately proposes child-friendly restricted phones and under-16 school phone bans.
