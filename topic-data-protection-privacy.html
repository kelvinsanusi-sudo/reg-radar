Data Protection & Privacy

Government trend & party positions, plus tracked documents in this area
UK data protection policy has shifted from the Conservative government's more contentious DPDI approach to Labour's Data (Use and Access) Act 2025, which received Royal Assent on 19 July 2025. The new framework reforms UK GDPR/DPA 2018 and introduces digital identity verification and a National Data Library, positioning data access as a tool for economic growth and improved public services.
Labour

Labour introduced the DUA Bill following the 2024 King's Speech and frames the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 as enabling economic growth and better public services whilst addressing criticisms of the Conservatives' prior approach.
